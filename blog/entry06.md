### Takeaways from my in-class presentation

My presentation in class was a little less nerve-wrecking than how the last time felt. I had prepared what I wanted to say in advance and recited it multiple times. This time I think I did a better job at that, so that's what eased my nerves more. Not only that, but I'm pretty sure I had more eye contact with people this time as well, which helped me keep more of the audience's attention. Although, I did need more details about my code, so I didn't get to talk about that. But to me, I think I atleast did better in other aspects of the presentation, I just need to remember to make sure I have a conclusion everytime.

### Takeaways from my EXPO Elevator Pitch

Giving my elevator pitch was surprisingly easier this time as well. It was scary to explain but since it came after the presentation, I had more clarity on what to talk about. I had a better time explaining my project and getting to talk about it to others, so I didn't get that worrying feeling this time. Before the pitch, I had also practiced what I was gonna say and tried to maybe change it a little, so that I'd have the main idea of what I'm getting across. This time I also think I was able to stay on track with the time limit I had, so I did good on that.
